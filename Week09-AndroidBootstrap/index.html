Android Tablets - ASUS ME173x Review

First unveiled at Computex 2013, Asus wowed the crowd when they announced the price of the MeMO Pad HD7 at $149 and $129. A new 7-inch Android tablet for such a low price? Asus is pushing the boundaries on what's possible. This is a great value tablet as you will see.

Through a stroke of brilliance, Professor Calvert was able to obtain mobile devices for us to play with in class. This improves our learning experience greatly. On this page you will find information about my experiences with an Asus Memo Pad (ASUS ME173x) at Bellevue College.

Specs:

  • OS: Android 4.2 Jelly Bean
  • Screen Size: 7 inches
  • Resolution: 1280 x 800 resolution IPS display
  • Processor: quad-core 1.2 GHz
  • RAM: 1 GB
  • Storage: 16GB storage (8GB available in emerging markets only)
  • Size: 196.8 x 120.6 x 10.8 mm (W x D x H)
  • GPS: yes

Below are just a few features of the Android Tablet. More images are on the Pictures tab. To see a full sized image, right click the image and choose View Image or Open image in new tab.

Account Settings. Integrate with Ease.

One of the best ideas with a mobile device is to integrate acess that allows the user to transfer data with ease. Within the Settings of the Android device, you can add a Google account so that you seamlessly integrate all of your contacts and pictures, storing them along with other information in the cloud.

This image (shown right) shows where to set this account for your mobile device.

Maps. Find your way.

On mobile devices that have GPS enabled, you can use the Maps application to navigate yourself to your destination. Depending on the signal that it recieves, the accuracy may be off slightly, but will get you there most of the time.

Tablet Applications. So many possibilities.

On this page you can see all of the installed applications you have on your tablet. The variety of what you can download and install is amazing. From games to business applications, as the saying goes... "there is an app for that"! You can list them either by Frequency of Use, Date Installed or Name.

The screenshot to the right shows applications installed on this tablet, with the most frequently used listed first. This setting is changed by clicking on the dropdown Sort By triangle located in the top right of the tablet screen.

WiFi. Connect to the cloud.

While your tablet can be used off-line, using WiFi with your tablet complements many of the features it offers, making it an extremely useful tool. To make sure that it is turned on, navigate to your Settings, WiFi Settings page (shown left). Here you can turn the WiFi on or off, find an available network connection, and see the status of your connection.

Once you are connected to a WiFi source, you can then utilize the endless capabilities of storing your data in the cloud, or even connecting to your work online. See the AirDroid or Mongo Database feature for more details on two of the endless options.

AirDroid Application. Manage Remotely.

The AirDroid application allows you to manage your Android tablet from your PC. More is covered on this application on the AirDroid tab.

Mongo Database. Do Work.

While connected wirelessly you can access multitudes of websites to do work remotely. One such service is Mongo Labs. They offer an online database connection service that allows you to store small databases in the cloud.

The screenshot to the left shows a user accessing their mongo database to edit some of the table data.
